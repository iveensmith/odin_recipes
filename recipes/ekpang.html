Ekpang Recipe

Ekpang

Ingredients

  • 1 kg water yam
  • 1.5 kg cocoyam
  • Perewinkles
  • leaves Ugu/green
  • Beef/goatmeat
  • Dry fish
  • Kpomo
  • Crayfish
  • Palm oil
  • cubes Seasoning
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • leaves Curry

Steps

  1. Peel,wash and grate or blend the coco-yam.
  2. Cut coco-yam leaves into small segments and wash.
  3. Top and tail periwinkle, this can actually be done for you in the market.
  4. Wash periwinkle thoroughly. It carries a lot of mud and sand.
  5. Place periwinkle at the base of pot. Add a little palm oil ,some pepper, salt and part of the seasoning.
  6. Mix the grated coco-yam with about Â½ cup of water to soften it further.
  7. Using your fingers,take about a teaspoon size of the paste at a time and wrap in the leaves placing gently in the pot. Place each wrap on the other until all the paste is wrapped.
  8. Place pot on the fire and allow to heat up .
  9. Add 2 cups of water from the edge of the pot a little at a time and allow to boil up and cook slowly until set
  10. Add the fish, shrimps , crabs, seasoning, pepper, utasi or ntong (scent leaves) and a little salt.
  11. Lower the heat and cook so that the Coco yam can through thoroughly .
  12. Shake the pot after about 20 minutes to loosen the food and to prevent from sticking to the base of the pot. Do not stir.
  13. Add remaining water and continue cooking for 10 minutes.
  14. Next add the crayfish and cook for another 5 minutes.
  15. Add the oil ,stir (actually lifting from side to side) with wooden spoon so the Ekpang nkwukwo is not all mushy.
  16. Taste and correct salt. Cook for 5 minutes.
  17. Serve hot
